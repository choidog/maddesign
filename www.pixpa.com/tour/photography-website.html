photography portfolio?

TIP 1

Have a clear vision

When it comes to building your photography portfolio, you must define a clear set of goals. What do you want your portfolio website to look like? What do you want your visitors and potential clients to see when they land on your website? Do you simply want a website for presenting your photography portfolio or do you want it to be more? How do you plan on using your photography portfolio website?

When you work on a big project for a client, your goal is to add value and contribute productively to their work or their life. Your website should perform the same function for you. Visualize a website that can contribute positively towards your creative career

Once you are clear on what kind of a website you want, you can start taking the necessary steps towards creating it. Browse through these amazing photography portfolios to get started. You might also want to read our step-by-step guide to becoming a professional photographer that would help you stand out from the crowd.

TIP 2

Be intentional and selective

When you have a proper vision in place, it's easier to be selective about what you showcase on your website. Always remember that less is more.

Be strategic with what you do and what you add. The idea is to effectively communicate your brand to customers.

Think about who your audience is. If you deal mainly with corporate clients, the kind of work you would showcase would be to attract those kinds of clients.

If you work with several different genres of photography, it is a good idea to section off your website to showcase your whole skillset and expertise. Plan your photography portfolio websites layout in a way that allows you to display your skills in different genres to clients in different industries.

TIP 3

Choose the right photography portfolio website template

You do not need any coding skills to build your own website, what you do need is the right photography portfolio website template.

Look for clean, easy to navigate and fast-loading photography website templates to ensure the best user experience. Also make sure that your website looks good on both desktop and mobile screens.The majority of web traffic on the internet today comes from mobile devices. TIP 4

Curation is key

Curate your best work and showcase only that in your photography portfolio. You may think, especially when youre starting out, that you need to show a lot of work to prove that you are experienced. However, a few excellent pieces are a much better representation of your expertise than several mediocre pieces. After all, you want to make a good first impression on potential clients and visitors.

Take a critical look at all of your work, especially your most recent work. Try to see which projects best showcase your expertise as a photographer, which photographs properly illustrate the length and breadth of your skillset and your range, and which images are the most aesthetically appealing to clients from a purely commercial perspective. Do read our article with great photography tips to help you get started.

Each piece you display on your photography portfolio website should fulfill a function. In other words, it should add value to your photography portfolio and to the story that you are trying to tell through it.

TIP 5

Keep it clean and simple

Less is always more. Dont overcrowd your photography portfolio, instead use white space to balance parts of the page. A clean, minimalist, easy-to-navigate website is much more user friendly and makes a much better first impression. See our guide on how to design your website.

Your website reflects your personal aesthetic taste, so create a photography portfolio website that you would be proud to show off to a client.

Think about your website as a whole. Keep all the different sections and pages of your website consistent in terms of color, design, theme etc. It's fun to experiment but dont go too overboard. Dont make a website that is so over complicated that it becomes confusing.

Always remember that, at its heart, website building is about storytelling. You want your website to be able to tell a story in an easy, compelling and most importantly comprehensible way.

You need your photography portfolio website to represent and reflect your brand. A personalized, custom website is a step forward towards creating your own brand. But you can always add more.

A custom domain name and a personalized brand logo can give your brand identity and make it more recognizable. Having your own personalized logo is also helpful because it is a visual representation of your business. Branding is an important process in improving your credibility and authority as a creative professional and business owner.

What should you have in your
photography portfolio website

What makes a photography portfolio website perfect? How can you make your portfolio stand out from the rest? It may all seem very challenging but it is really not. Here is a list of things you should consider including in your photography portfolio website.

  • 1. A beautiful landing page

    What do you want someone to see when they click on your website for the very first time? A beautiful, modern landing page is absolutely essential when you are looking to make a good first impression. After all, you want to turn visitors into clients and for that, impressions matter.

  • 2. An About page

    This is your opportunity to forge a personal connection with potential clients. Put yourself out there. Communicate through your work but dont forget the human behind the lens. A good headshot, a short but authentic biographical note as well as a statement on your artistic philosophy and creative process are must-have additions to this page. Do read our article with great tips on how to write content for your website.

  • 3. Your best curated work

    Your photography portfolio website should only feature your best work. Curate pieces that show your skills and expertise in a wide range of lighting conditions, locations, and genres. Also make sure that you are selecting recent work or at the very least work that fits your current style and accurately represents the kind of work you are doing now.

  • 4. Separate galleries for different categories

    If you are a photographer who works in different genres, your photography portfolio should reflect that. However, make sure you create different galleries or albums for different categories. This makes your website neat, coherent and easy to navigate. User experience matters.

  • 5. Client galleries

    With client galleries you can create dedicated private or public galleries for each individual client. Client galleries can be used not just for sharing and proofing your work but also for making sales. These galleries offer exclusivity and convenience for both you and your clients making them an amazing selling point for your photography business.

  • 6. A contact form

    Make sure you have a contact form on your photography portfolio website so that potential clients can contact you directly for enquiries and bookings. After all, your website is supposed to be an easy one stop shop for you to grow your business. No one likes jumping through hoops. A contact form is an easy and convenient way of converting site visitors into paying clients.

Photography portfolio Website FAQs

What is a photography portfolio website?

Your photography portfolio is a collection of your best, curated work. Your photography portfolio is an important tool that lets you share your photographs and resume with potential clients and employers and show them your expertise, technical skills,method and style. While earlier it was common to carry big, physical copies of your portfolio, these days hosting your photography portfolio on your own custom website is easier and enables you to reach a wider audience.

Why is a photography portfolio website important?

An online portfolio website saves you the trouble of sharing materials, samples and resumes individually, as you can simply link your website with potential clients.
